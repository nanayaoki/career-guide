Microbiology

Microbiology studies bacteria, viruses, fungi, and other microorganisms. It is important in healthcare, research, food safety, pharmaceuticals, and biotechnology.

Introduction

What this field is

Microbiology focuses on tiny living organisms and their role in human health, medicine, agriculture, and industry. Students often move toward lab-based careers, research, and healthcare-related specializations.

Study Requirements

Path after 10th

  • 12th Science stream with Biology or PCM/PCB depending on the university path.
  • Degrees like B.Sc Microbiology, B.Sc Biotechnology, B.Sc Biochemistry, or related courses.
  • For advanced roles, students can pursue M.Sc Microbiology or research-oriented programs.
Exams Required

Entrance options

  • NEET for medical and allied health routes where applicable.
  • University entrances such as CUET or state-level entrance tests.
  • Institutional interviews or merit-based admissions for some B.Sc programs.
Career Opportunities

Popular jobs

  • Microbiologist
  • Lab Technician
  • Clinical Research Assistant
  • Quality Control Analyst
  • Biotech Research Associate
